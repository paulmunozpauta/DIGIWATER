DIGIWATER: Building Resilient Water Futures

About DIGIWATER

During a meeting in March 2025 at VUB with the coordinators of the Strategic Institutional Partnerships (SIP) with the University of the Western Cape (UWC), South Africa, and Universidad Central de Las Villas (UCLV), Cuba, along with UCLV’s rector, a shared interest in strengthening interdisciplinary collaborations on water challenges was identified. DIGIWATER responds to that vision, using the International Scientific Symposium CCI2025, hosted by UCLV in October 2025, as a launchpad for joint action.

DIGIWATER expands the SIP framework and connects VUB to three additional strategic partners—Universidad de Cuenca (UCUENCA, Ecuador), Universidad Católica Boliviana (UCB) and Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú (PUCP)—through two interdisciplinary workshops:

  • Water Resilience through Citizen Science, focused on inclusive governance and water justice
  • Water Resilience through Digitisation, exploring IoT, AI, and data tools for water applications

In addition, two training courses targeting Cuban early career scientists and staff will focus on SWAT modelling and Digitisation and AI for water applications. These activities promote knowledge exchange, research decolonisation, and collaborative research to build resilient water futures in the Global South through digital tools and citizen engagement—key priorities in the VLIR-UOS country reference frameworks for Cuba, Bolivia, Ecuador, and Peru.

DIGIWATER also aligns with the UNESCO Intergovernmental Hydrological Programme (IHP) and the Chair on Open Water Science and Education, hosted at VUB and chaired by Prof. Ann Van Griensven, which promotes open science through open data, IoT, and online education. A short video will document highlights from the activities. All outputs will be shared via the project website and VUB channels to boost VUB visibility.
